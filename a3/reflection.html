Group Reflection

What Went Well?

In assignment 2 the group reflected that keeping a track of project tasks, areas of responsibility and project timelines was challenging and messy. So, for assignment 3 we implemented a project planned in Teams, and Katherine took on a role of part time project manager. This worked very well, as each team member could access the board to get an overview of tasks at any time, some responsibility is on group member to keep the board updated, and Katherine oversaw task completion as a whole and pushed timeframes to remain on target.

What Could be Improved?

Group communication is the key point that the group has identified could be improved to ultimately produce a higher quality of work in the final submission. The group recognizes that some aspects of communication have improved since Assignment 2; meeting scheduling, meeting agendas and actions were consistently done during Assignment 3. However, the group struggled to communicate effectively via teams in between calls and some members did not regularly update the project board with their progress, meaning the rest of the group had no visibility on what work was getting done.

Something that was Surprising?

Despite the challenges, the group as a whole reflects that the group process overall was easier in Assignment 3. This is surprising because the timeline was tight and there were challenges, but overall there was less stress in the group in meeting the deadline. We reflect that this is because the group has built some working relationship and come to understand one another’s working style. Also, a level of trust has been built, so group members have some confidence that their team mates will get the work done.

One thing we learned about working in groups

Our group was made up of individuals with drastically different personalities, interests, and levels of IT skills. Some members were quiet and reserved, while others were more dominant and vocal. It seemed impossible to find common ground at first, but as we worked together, we learned to appreciate the unique strengths of each team member. We learned that even the most contrasting personalities could come together and complement each other to produce a high-quality end result. The key to our success was effective leadership and clear communication. Our project leader, Katherine, was crucial in keeping us organised and on track. We may have started as strangers with different goals and motivations, but by the end of the project, we had become a cohesive team that was proud of what we had accomplished.
